Introduction

The contest will last 2 months so until (insert end date). Through this contest we want to promote creativity and originality with Game One and Game Two. We joined forces with a professional in that field who publish games for PSVita in order to also help amateur developers to grow up and improve their skills with critics from an expert in that field. In the past (PSP era mostly, alongside PS3 era), the hacking community was at its peak with an active community, forums and legends from the scene (DarK Alex, or geohot to name a few) fueled us with magic on our hands and raised a large interest into hacking their Sony devices. As time goes further, with devices like PS4 and PS5 and the security being tougher and tougher, the hacking community activity decreased with the loss of important websites like qj.net or pspgen. This contest, with the involvement of Wololo, PSX-Place and PlaystationHaX is the occasion to light up that lost fire in our community and hopefully raise the interest about hacking their device again. This contest is born from the will of the community to have one after the recent improvement, and we are making it into a reality. But for it to be successful we need you!

The Process

The contest will proceed via 3 simple steps:

1. Each participant will submit their projects into the 4 categories listed below. The contest will last 2 months from [add date]

2. The jury will judge the projects and make their own ranking per category, we will merge those rankings to make an overall ranking assessment.

3. The prize pool will be split according to the prize share chart that you can find in the rules and info section.

The Four Catagories

Game One

• Create a game with an existing engine of choise, for example GameMaker or Unity

Game Two

• Create a game with your own engine, for example vitaSDK or dolceSDK

Utility

Ports

• Create a game with your own engine, for example vitaSDK or dolceSDK
• Probabo, inquit, modo dixi, constituto, ut earum rerum facilis est.

The Voting

The jury will individually submit a post explaining how they made their ranking which will cover what they liked, disliked, any idea of improvement and advices for the said project.

A general ranking will be made with all the jurors ranking. Result will be displayed in the contest website.

Rules

1 Developers can submit as many submissions as they want but can only win one prize per category.

2 For games, if they were previously released on another platform like PS4 or Switch, even if it is yours it must be submitted in the port category. Utility from other homebrew scenes shall remain in the utility category though.

3 If the game you want to submit was already released before on PSVita, it must have been at the state of prototype and being improved to make it a complete game.

4 No piracy will be allowed. No use of copyrighted assets is accepted, games must be the author original creation. As for ports, do not include game files if they are not free.

5 You must have a paypal account to receive the money.

6 You must include the contest splash screen in the booting process of your homebrew (Which will be selected during the 15 days of the contest)

7 Your project must be open source (Game One participants are not obliged to).

8 If we catch you cheating, you are automatically banned from the contest
